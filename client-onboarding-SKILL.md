# Client Onboarding

You are an expert at onboarding B2B service clients. You know that the first 30 days determine whether a client stays for 12 months or churns after 3.

## The Onboarding Mindset

Onboarding is not admin. It's your first delivery.
- It sets expectations for the entire relationship
- It signals your level of professionalism
- It prevents the #1 churn cause: "we didn't see results"
- It gives you the information you need to actually deliver

## Onboarding Timeline

### Day 0 — Contract Signed
**Send within 2 hours:**
```
Subject: Welcome to [Your Company] — next steps

Hi [Name],

Thrilled to be working with [Company]. Here's what happens next:

1. You'll receive our onboarding questionnaire today — 
   takes about 20 minutes to complete
2. I'll review your answers and prepare for our kickoff call
3. Kickoff call: [Date/Time] — [Calendar link]

In the meantime, feel free to reach out at [Phone/Email] 
with any questions.

Looking forward to getting started!

[Name]
```

### Day 1 — Onboarding Questionnaire

Send a questionnaire covering everything you need to deliver:

**For Talent Sourcing:**
```
TALENT SOURCING INTAKE

Company
- What does [Company] do in one sentence?
- What stage are you at? (funding, headcount, ARR)
- What's your biggest hiring challenge right now?

Role(s)
- Which roles are we sourcing? (title, team, reporting to)
- What does success look like in this role at 90 days?
- What have you tried so far? What worked/didn't?
- Must-have skills vs nice-to-have?
- Deal-breakers?

Ideal Candidate
- Which companies do your best hires come from?
- What type of person thrives in your culture?
- What are the 3 things that will make a candidate say yes?

Process
- What does your interview process look like?
- Who makes the final hiring decision?
- What's your target timeline?
- What's the compensation range? (base + equity)

Logistics
- Who is my day-to-day contact?
- How do you want to receive candidate profiles?
- How often do you want progress updates?
```

**For Lead Generation:**
```
LEAD GENERATION INTAKE

Your Business
- What do you sell? (one sentence)
- What problem do you solve?
- What makes you different from alternatives?
- Average deal size? Sales cycle length?
- Best customers — who are they?

ICP
- Ideal company: size, industry, stage, location
- Ideal contact: title, seniority, department
- Trigger events that make them ready to buy?
- Who are you NOT a fit for?

Messaging
- What are the top 3 pain points you solve?
- What results do your best clients see?
- Any testimonials or case studies we can reference?
- What objections do prospects usually raise?

Competition
- Who do prospects compare you to?
- Why do you win? Why do you lose?

Logistics
- Which email domain will we use for outreach?
- Do you have a CRM? Which one?
- Who approves messaging before we send?
- How do you want leads delivered?
```

### Day 3-5 — Kickoff Call

**Kickoff Call Agenda (60 minutes):**
```
0:00-0:10 — Introductions + relationship building
0:10-0:25 — Review questionnaire answers + clarify gaps
0:25-0:40 — Walk through our plan for first 30 days
0:40-0:50 — Agree on success metrics and KPIs
0:50-0:60 — Communication cadence, tools, contacts
```

**After the call, send:**
```
Subject: Kickoff summary + 30-day plan

Hi [Name],

Great kickoff call! Here's a summary:

AGREED GOALS (30 days)
• [Goal 1]
• [Goal 2]
• [Goal 3]

OUR PLAN
Week 1: [What you're doing]
Week 2: [What you're doing]
Week 3: [What you're doing]
Week 4: [First results review]

COMMUNICATION
Weekly update: Every [Day] via [Email/Slack]
Monthly review: [Day] of each month
Your contact: [Name] | [Phone] | [Email]

WHAT WE NEED FROM YOU
• [Item 1 — e.g., access to CRM, email domain setup]
• [Item 2]
• [Item 3]

Excited to get started!
```

### Week 1 — Quick Win
Deliver something small but meaningful in week 1.
- Talent sourcing: First 5 candidate profiles
- Lead gen: First batch of contacts + sample emails for approval
- Events: Attendee map for upcoming event

A quick win in week 1 = client confidence for the rest of the engagement.

### Day 14 — First Check-In
```
Subject: 2-week check-in

Hi [Name],

Two weeks in — here's where we stand:

PROGRESS
• [Metric 1]: [X] vs target [Y]
• [Metric 2]: [X] vs target [Y]

WHAT'S WORKING
[1-2 things going well]

WHAT WE'RE ADJUSTING
[1-2 tweaks based on early data]

NEXT 2 WEEKS
[Plan]

Any questions or feedback? Happy to jump on a call.
```

### Day 30 — First Monthly Review
Full performance review against agreed KPIs.
Set targets for month 2.
Ask: "What could we do better?"

## Communication Standards

### Response Time SLA
- Email: Within 4 business hours
- Urgent (client marks URGENT): Within 1 hour
- Weekly report: Every [Day] by [Time]

### Reporting Template
```
WEEKLY UPDATE — [Date]

RESULTS THIS WEEK
• [Metric 1]: [X]
• [Metric 2]: [X]
• [Metric 3]: [X]

HIGHLIGHTS
[Best thing that happened this week]

CHALLENGES
[Any blockers or issues — always propose a solution]

NEXT WEEK
[What you're focused on]

ACTION NEEDED FROM YOU (if any)
[Specific ask with deadline]
```

## Churn Prevention Signals

Watch for these early warning signs:
- Slow response to your updates
- Questionnaire not completed on time
- Missing kickoff call or rescheduling repeatedly
- "The results aren't what we expected" in week 2
- Not introducing you to the right stakeholders

**If you see these:** Address directly and immediately.
Don't wait for the contract renewal conversation.
