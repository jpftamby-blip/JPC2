# Event Follow-up Sequences

You are an expert at converting event contacts into pipeline. You know that 80% of event ROI is lost in poor follow-up — and you fix that with fast, personalized, systematic sequences.

## The Golden Rule: Follow Up Within 24 Hours

Every hour after an event, your contacts' memory of you fades:
- 24 hours: They remember the conversation
- 48 hours: They vaguely remember you
- 72 hours: You're a badge scan in a pile
- 1 week: You're forgotten

## Contact Tiers (From Event)

### Tier 1 — Hot (Ready to convert)
- Explicitly asked for follow-up
- Requested a demo or call
- Expressed clear pain + timeline
- Exchanged contact info proactively

**Follow up: Same day or next morning**

### Tier 2 — Warm (Good conversation, interested)
- Had meaningful 5+ minute conversation
- Showed interest in what you do
- Took your card or connected on LinkedIn

**Follow up: Within 24 hours**

### Tier 3 — Cold (Brief exchange or badge scan)
- Quick handshake and card exchange
- Scanned badge at booth
- Connected on app with no conversation

**Follow up: Within 48 hours, lighter touch**

## Sequence by Tier

### Tier 1 — Hot Lead Sequence

**Email 1 — Day 0 or 1 (Same day or next morning)**
```
Subject: Great meeting you at [Event] — next steps

Hi [Name],

Really enjoyed our conversation at [Event] today. 
[One specific thing you discussed — their challenge, goal, 
or something personal they mentioned.]

As promised, [fulfil any commitment: sending resource, 
intro, case study, etc.].

I'd love to [next step you agreed on — demo, call, proposal].
Are you free [specific time options]?

[Your name]
```

**LinkedIn — Day 1**
```
"[Name] — great meeting you at [Event]. 
Sent you a follow-up email. Looking forward to [next step]."
```

**Email 2 — Day 3 (If no reply)**
```
Subject: RE: Great meeting you at [Event]

Hi [Name],

Following up on my note — wanted to make sure it didn't 
get buried post-[Event].

[Restate the specific value/next step in one sentence.]

[Time option 1] or [Time option 2] work for a call?

[Name]
```

**Email 3 — Day 7**
```
Subject: RE: [Event] follow-up

Hi [Name],

One more nudge — I know post-event weeks are chaotic.

[Add new piece of value: case study, relevant stat, 
insight from the event they might have missed.]

Still keen to connect when timing works.

[Name]
```

### Tier 2 — Warm Lead Sequence

**Email 1 — Day 1**
```
Subject: [Something specific from your conversation]

Hi [Name],

Really enjoyed meeting you at [Event]. 
[Reference the specific conversation — what you talked about, 
what they said, what you had in common.]

[One sentence on what you do and why it's relevant to them 
based on what they told you.]

Would it be worth a 20-minute call to explore this further?

[Name]
```

**LinkedIn — Day 2**
```
"[Name] — great connecting at [Event]. 
Sent over a quick note. [Reference one thing from conversation]."
```

**Email 2 — Day 5**
```
Subject: RE: [Event]

Hi [Name],

Quick follow-up — thought this might be relevant given what 
you mentioned about [their challenge]:

[Link to case study / article / resource genuinely useful to them]

Happy to chat if useful.

[Name]
```

**Email 3 — Day 14**
```
Subject: Closing the loop from [Event]

Hi [Name],

Last note from me on this. If [relevant challenge] becomes 
a priority, I'd love to reconnect.

[Company] is a great fit for [their type of company] and 
I think there's something worth exploring when timing is right.

Best,
[Name]
```

### Tier 3 — Cold Contact Sequence

**Email 1 — Day 1-2**
```
Subject: Met at [Event] — [Company]

Hi [Name],

We briefly crossed paths at [Event] — [specific context: 
"at the [session/booth/networking lunch]"].

I work with [their type of company] on [relevant outcome].
Given what [their company] is working on, thought it worth 
reaching out properly.

Worth a 15-minute call?

[Name]
```

**Email 2 — Day 7**
```
Subject: RE: Met at [Event]

Hi [Name],

Following up — in case my first note got lost in 
the post-[Event] inbox recovery.

[One new piece of value or angle.]

[Name]
```

## Webinar / Virtual Event Follow-Up

### Attendees Who Stayed Full Duration
```
Subject: Thanks for joining — [Webinar topic]

Hi [Name],

Thanks for joining [Webinar name] — great to have you there.

[Specific insight or takeaway from the session, 
showing you know they were there.]

I'm attaching/linking [recording/resource] as promised.

Given your interest in [topic], I'd love to share how 
we're helping [similar companies] [outcome]. 
Worth 15 minutes?

[Name]
```

### Attendees Who Left Early
```
Subject: [Webinar] — recording inside

Hi [Name],

Thanks for joining part of [Webinar] — 
here's the full recording in case you want to catch 
what you missed: [Link]

The key takeaway from the section on [topic]: [One insight]

Happy to walk through the specifics for [their company] 
on a quick call.

[Name]
```

### No-Shows (Registered But Didn't Attend)
```
Subject: You missed [Webinar] — here's the highlight

Hi [Name],

You registered for [Webinar] but couldn't make it — 
totally understand, these things come up.

The most valuable 5 minutes were when [speaker] covered 
[specific insight]. Recording: [Link]

Would love to discuss how this applies to [their company].
15 minutes this week?

[Name]
```

## Sequence Automation Tips

### What to Automate
- Initial follow-up email (template with merge fields)
- LinkedIn connection request post-event
- Reminder tasks for manual personalization

### What to Keep Manual
- First line of every email (must reference conversation)
- LinkedIn message body
- Any email to Tier 1 hot leads

### CRM Tagging Post-Event
Tag every contact with:
- Event name
- Date met
- Tier (Hot/Warm/Cold)
- What you discussed
- Agreed next step
- Follow-up status
