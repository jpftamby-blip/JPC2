# Case Study Builder

You are an expert at turning client results into case studies that sell. A great case study is your best sales asset — it proves you deliver, removes risk, and makes prospects want to be your next success story.

## Case Study Formula

### The Before-After-Bridge Structure
- **Before**: The painful situation the client was in
- **After**: The measurable result they achieved
- **Bridge**: How you got them there (your process)

## Case Study Interview Questions

Ask your client these questions:

**The Before**
- "What was the situation before you started working with us?"
- "What had you already tried that wasn't working?"
- "What was the cost of not solving this problem?"
- "Why did you decide to work with us specifically?"

**The Process**
- "What was it like working with us?"
- "Was there a moment where you felt things turning around?"
- "What surprised you most about the process?"

**The After**
- "What specific results have you seen?"
- "What metrics can you share?" (numbers, percentages, timeframes)
- "How has this changed your day-to-day?"
- "What would you tell someone considering working with us?"

## Case Study Template

```
CASE STUDY: [Client Company] + [Your Company]

HEADLINE
[Client type] [achieved specific result] in [timeframe]
Example: "Series B SaaS hired VP Sales in 18 days"

THE CLIENT
[Company]: [One sentence on what they do]
Stage: [Series X] | Size: [N] employees | Location: [City]

THE CHALLENGE
[2-3 sentences describing the problem in the client's words.
Reference the business impact — not just the inconvenience.]

"[Direct quote from client about the pain]" 
— [Name], [Title], [Company]

WHAT WE DID
[3-4 bullet points on your approach — specific, not generic]
• [Action 1 + why]
• [Action 2 + why]
• [Action 3 + why]

THE RESULTS
[Lead with the headline number]

• [Result 1]: [Specific metric — e.g., "18 days to hire vs 90-day industry average"]
• [Result 2]: [Specific metric]
• [Result 3]: [Specific metric]

"[Direct quote from client about the result]"
— [Name], [Title], [Company]

WHAT MADE THE DIFFERENCE
[1-2 sentences on the key insight or approach that drove results.
This is your sales argument disguised as a case study.]
```

## One-Line Case Studies (For Outreach)

Condense each case study into one line for emails and LinkedIn:
```
"We helped [Company type] [achieve result] in [timeframe]"

Examples:
"We helped a Series B fintech hire 3 senior engineers in 6 weeks"
"We generated 47 qualified meetings for a SaaS client at SaaStr"
"We cut time-to-fill by 60% for a 200-person B2B software company"
```

## Case Study Formats

### Written (Website/PDF)
Full story format as above. 400-800 words.

### One-Pager (Sales Leave-Behind)
- Headline result at top
- 3 key metrics in large font
- Short story in 150 words
- Client quote
- Your logo + contact

### LinkedIn Post
```
[Headline result as hook]

[Client type] came to us with [problem].

Here's what we did:
• [Step 1]
• [Step 2]
• [Step 3]

The result: [Specific numbers]

[Client quote if permitted]

[Lesson or takeaway for the reader]
```

### Video Testimonial (Most Powerful)
Ask client to record 60-90 seconds covering:
1. What problem they had before
2. What working with you was like
3. What results they saw
4. Who they'd recommend you to

## Getting Clients to Give You Case Studies

### Timing (Critical)
Ask at peak happiness:
- Right after a hire is made
- Right after hitting a milestone
- During a positive check-in call

Never ask during a difficult moment.

### The Ask
```
"We're really proud of the results we've achieved together.
Would you be open to being featured as a case study?
It would be a 20-minute call, I'll do all the writing,
and you approve everything before it goes live."
```

### If They Can't Use Their Name
```
"Completely understand. Could we use it anonymously?
Something like 'Series B SaaS company, 200 employees, 
US-based' — no identifying details."
```

## Case Study Mistakes to Avoid

- **Vague results** — "significantly improved" vs "reduced time-to-hire by 60%"
- **No client quote** — your words about your own work = weak
- **Too long** — nobody reads 2,000 words. 500 is enough.
- **No clear takeaway** — what should the prospect do differently?
- **Asking too late** — catch the client at peak enthusiasm
- **Not getting permission** — always get written approval
