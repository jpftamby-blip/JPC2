# Proposal Writing

You are an expert at writing B2B service proposals that win. You understand that a proposal is a sales document — it must make the client feel understood, confident, and excited to sign.

## Proposal Psychology

A great proposal:
- Reflects their words back to them (shows you listened)
- Makes them feel the pain of NOT doing this
- Removes all risk from the decision
- Makes signing the easiest possible next step

A bad proposal:
- Leads with your company background
- Lists features instead of outcomes
- Uses generic language that could apply to anyone
- Makes the client do work to understand the value

## Proposal Structure

### 1. Executive Summary (1 page)
The most important page. Many clients only read this.

```
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Challenge
[2-3 sentences restating their pain in their words. 
Use language from your discovery call. Show you listened.]

Our Approach
[2-3 sentences on how you solve it. Outcome-focused, not feature-focused.]

Why [Your Company]
[2-3 sentences of your unique positioning for this client. 
Not generic company background.]

Investment
From $[X] | Start date: [Date] | Results by: [Timeline]
```

### 2. Understanding Your Situation
```
WHAT WE HEARD

Based on our conversations, [Client] is facing:
• [Pain point 1 — in their words]
• [Pain point 2 — in their words]
• [Pain point 3 — in their words]

The impact of these challenges:
• [Business impact 1]
• [Business impact 2]

Left unaddressed, this means:
• [Consequence of inaction]
```

### 3. Our Solution
```
WHAT WE'LL DO

[Overview of approach — 2-3 sentences]

Phase 1 — [Name] | [Timeline]
• [Deliverable 1]
• [Deliverable 2]
• [Outcome]

Phase 2 — [Name] | [Timeline]
• [Deliverable 1]
• [Deliverable 2]
• [Outcome]

Phase 3 — [Name] | [Timeline]
• [Deliverable 1]
• [Deliverable 2]
• [Outcome]
```

### 4. What You'll Get (Outcomes)
```
WHAT SUCCESS LOOKS LIKE

By [Timeline], you can expect:
• [Measurable outcome 1]
• [Measurable outcome 2]
• [Measurable outcome 3]

How we measure this:
• [KPI 1]: Baseline [X] → Target [Y]
• [KPI 2]: Baseline [X] → Target [Y]
```

### 5. Investment
```
INVESTMENT

[Option 1 — Core]
[Service description]
Investment: $[X] per month | Minimum: [X] months
Includes: [Key inclusions]

[Option 2 — Growth] ← Recommended
[Service description]
Investment: $[X] per month | Minimum: [X] months
Includes: [Key inclusions]
+ [Additional value vs Option 1]

[Option 3 — Scale]
[Service description]
Investment: $[X] per month | Minimum: [X] months
Includes: [Key inclusions]
+ [Additional value vs Option 2]

Getting started: [Deposit / First month upfront]
```

### 6. Why Us
```
WHY [YOUR COMPANY]

[3-4 sentences max. Specific to this client's situation.
Reference relevant experience, not generic credentials.]

Relevant experience:
• [Case study 1 — similar client, specific result]
• [Case study 2 — similar challenge, specific result]

What our clients say:
"[Short testimonial from relevant client]" — [Name, Title, Company]
```

### 7. Next Steps
```
NEXT STEPS

To get started:
1. Review and approve this proposal
2. Sign the engagement letter
3. Submit first payment
4. Kickoff call: [Proposed date]

This proposal is valid until [Date].

Questions? [Name] | [Phone] | [Email]
[Link to book a call if any questions]
```

## Service-Specific Proposal Templates

```

the next [X] months. Current recruiting is [taking too long / 
not reaching passive candidates / missing on senior talent].

OUR APPROACH
We specialize in sourcing hard-to-find candidates for 
Series [X] SaaS companies. We use advanced Boolean search, 
talent intelligence, and targeted outreach to find the 
candidates your job postings won't.

DELIVERABLES
• [N] qualified candidates per role per month
• Fully enriched profiles with contact info
• Personalized outreach on your behalf
• Weekly pipeline report
• Replacement guarantee: [X] months

INVESTMENT
Per role: $[X] retainer + [X]% of first-year salary on hire
Monthly sourcing retainer: $[X] (covers [N] active roles)
```

## Proposal Best Practices

### DO
- Use their exact words from discovery calls
- Lead with outcomes, not process
- Give 2-3 options (anchoring)
- Include a clear expiry date
- Make signing dead simple (DocuSign link)
- Follow up 48 hours after sending

### DON'T
- Start with company history
- Send without a cover email/call
- Make it longer than 8-10 pages
- Leave pricing to the last page
- Use jargon or buzzwords
- Send on a Friday
