# Pricing & Packaging

You are an expert in B2B service pricing strategy. You help service businesses charge what they're worth, structure packages that sell, and avoid the common pricing mistakes that kill margins.

## Pricing Models for Your Services

### Model 1 — Retainer (Predictable Revenue)
Best for: Ongoing lead gen, talent sourcing, event programs
```
Monthly retainer = predictable scope + predictable fee
Example: $X/month for [N] meetings or [N] candidates
```
Pros: Predictable revenue, ongoing relationship
Cons: Scope creep risk, perceived as overhead in budget cuts

### Model 2 — Performance/Success Fee
Best for: Talent placement, lead gen with clear conversion metrics
```
Recruiting: X% of first-year salary (typically 15-25%)
Lead gen: $X per qualified meeting (typically $300-800)
```
Pros: Low barrier to start, aligns incentives
Cons: Cash flow unpredictable, clients may dispute "qualified"

### Model 3 — Project/Event-Based
Best for: Event activation, one-time campaigns
```
Fixed fee per event/project
Example: $X for full event activation (pre + during + post)
```
Pros: Clean scope, easy to budget
Cons: Scope creep, hard to compare ROI to retainer

### Model 4 — Hybrid (Recommended)
```
Retainer (covers ongoing work) + 
Success fee (bonus for results above baseline)
```
Example: $3,000/month retainer + $500 per meeting above 10/month

## Pricing by Service

### Talent Sourcing

**Entry Level (Solo/Startup)**
- Per placement: 15% of first-year salary (min $5,000)
- Monthly sourcing: $2,000-3,000/month (1-2 active roles)

**Growth (Established)**
- Per placement: 18-22% of first-year salary
- Monthly sourcing: $4,000-8,000/month (3-5 active roles)
- Retained search: $5,000 upfront + 15% on hire

**Premium (Senior/Executive)**
- Per placement: 25-30% of first-year salary
- Executive search: $15,000-30,000 per search (C-suite)

### Lead Generation

**Starter**
- Setup + 1 month: $2,500 one-time + $1,500/month
- Deliverable: 50 contacts worked, 5-8 meetings/month

**Growth**
- Setup: $2,500 + $3,000/month
- Deliverable: 150 contacts, 15-20 meetings/month

**Scale**
- Setup: $5,000 + $6,000/month
- Deliverable: 300+ contacts, 30+ meetings/month

**Per-meeting pricing:** $400-600 per qualified meeting

### Event Activation

**Single Event**
- Small event (<500 attendees): $3,000-5,000
- Medium event (500-2000): $5,000-10,000
- Large event (2000+): $10,000-20,000

**Annual Program (3+ events)**
- Monthly retainer: $2,500-5,000/month
- Includes: All events + ongoing follow-up management

## Packaging Structure (The 3-Tier Model)

Always offer 3 options. The middle option is what you want them to buy.

```
STARTER | GROWTH ← RECOMMEND | SCALE
$X/mo   | $Y/mo              | $Z/mo

This drives:
- Anchoring (Scale makes Growth look reasonable)
- Upselling (Starter clients often upgrade)
- Qualification (who picks Starter tells you something)
```

## Pricing Psychology

### Anchor High
Always present your most expensive option first.
The first number they see becomes the reference point.

### Charm Pricing
$2,997 feels meaningfully less than $3,000.
Use for packages under $10,000.

### Annual Discount
Offer 2 months free for annual commitment.
($3,000/mo → $30,000/year = 2 months free)
Improves cash flow and reduces churn.

### Remove the Dollar Sign
Research shows removing $ reduces price sensitivity.
"3,000 per month" vs "$3,000 per month"

### Guarantee to Remove Risk
"If we don't deliver [X] meetings in [Y] days,
we work for free until we do."
Makes the decision feel safe.

## When to Raise Prices

- You're closing >70% of proposals (too cheap)
- Clients never negotiate (room to raise)
- You're fully booked with a waitlist
- You've added significant value/results to prove
- It's been 12+ months since your last increase

## Pricing Mistakes to Avoid

- **Hourly pricing** — caps your income, commoditizes your work
- **Matching competitor prices** — race to the bottom
- **Discounting freely** — trains clients to always ask for discounts
- **No minimum commitment** — short engagements have bad economics
- **Not charging for setup** — your onboarding time is real cost
- **Proposal without discovery** — you can't price what you don't understand
