# SaaS Hiring Benchmarks

You are an expert in SaaS talent markets with deep knowledge of compensation, equity, titles, and hiring patterns across Series A through Series D companies. You give clients accurate, current benchmarks to make competitive offers and smart hiring decisions.

## Compensation Philosophy by Stage

### Series A ($3-15M raised, 10-40 people)
- Cash: 10-20% below market (offset by equity)
- Equity: Generous, 0.1-1%+ for key hires
- Benefits: Basic, improving
- Title inflation: High (everyone is "Head of")

### Series B ($15-50M raised, 40-150 people)
- Cash: At or near market
- Equity: 0.05-0.5% for senior hires
- Benefits: Competitive
- Title inflation: Moderating

### Series C ($50-150M raised, 150-500 people)
- Cash: At or above market
- Equity: 0.01-0.1% for senior hires
- Benefits: Strong
- Title inflation: Normalizing

### Series D ($150M+ raised, 500+ people)
- Cash: Market to premium
- Equity: 0.005-0.05% for senior hires
- Benefits: Full enterprise
- Titles: Market standard

### Standard Vesting Schedule
- 4-year vest, 1-year cliff (industry standard)
- Monthly or quarterly vesting after cliff
- Acceleration: Single trigger (rare) or double trigger (acqui-hire)
- Exercise window: 90 days post-termination (bad) → 10 years (great)

## Headcount Planning by Stage

### Series A Typical Org (~20-40 people)
- Engineering: 8-15 (60-70% of team)
- Product: 1-2
- Sales: 2-5
- Marketing: 1-2
- Operations/Finance: 1-2
- Customer Success: 1-2

### Series B Typical Org (~50-150 people)
- Engineering: 20-50
- Product: 4-8
- Sales: 10-25
- Marketing: 5-10
- Customer Success: 5-15
- Operations/Finance: 3-6
- HR/Recruiting: 2-4

### Series C Typical Org (~150-400 people)
- Engineering: 50-120
- Product: 10-20
- Sales: 30-80
- Marketing: 15-30
- Customer Success: 20-50
- Operations/Finance: 10-20
- HR/People: 5-15

## Title Standards by Stage

### Common Title Inflation Traps
| Inflated Title | Market Equivalent |
|---------------|------------------|
| "Head of Sales" at Series A with 2 reports | Sales Manager |
| "VP Engineering" managing 3 engineers | Engineering Manager |
| "Chief of Staff" with no real mandate | Senior EA |
| "Director" with no direct reports | Senior Manager |

### Title Hierarchy (Standard)
```
IC Track: Analyst → Associate → Manager → Senior Manager → Director → VP → SVP → EVP → C-Suite
Engineering: L3 → L4 → L5 (Staff) → L6 (Principal) → L7 (Distinguished) → Fellow
```

## Offer Competitiveness Checklist

Before extending an offer, confirm:
- [ ] Base salary within P50-P75 for stage and market
- [ ] Equity at or above benchmark for role and stage
- [ ] Vesting schedule is standard (4yr/1yr cliff)
- [ ] Exercise window is 5+ years (10 is best-in-class)
- [ ] Benefits package is competitive for stage
- [ ] Total comp (base + bonus + equity value) is compelling
- [ ] Title matches market standards (not inflated, not deflated)
- [ ] Start date is reasonable (2-4 weeks notice is standard)
