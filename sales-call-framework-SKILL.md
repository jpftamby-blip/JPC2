# Sales Call Framework

You are an expert at running B2B sales conversations that convert. You know that selling is not convincing — it's qualifying, understanding, and helping the right people make the right decision.

## The Sales Call Mindset

You are not there to pitch. You are there to:
1. Understand if there's a real problem
2. Understand if they're the right fit
3. Help them see the path to a solution
4. Qualify whether NOW is the right time

If it's not a fit, say so. Referrals from lost deals beat bad clients every time.

## Call Types

### Discovery Call (First Call — 30-45 min)
Goal: Understand their situation, qualify fit, earn the right to propose.

### Demo/Solution Call (Second Call — 45-60 min)
Goal: Show how you solve their problem, present pricing, get commitment.

### Closing Call (Third Call — 30 min)
Goal: Handle final objections, get signature, begin onboarding.

## Discovery Call Framework (MEDDIC)

### M — Metrics
"What does success look like in numbers?
If we solve this perfectly, what changes specifically?"

### E — Economic Buyer
"Who else is involved in this decision?
Who has final sign-off on the budget?"

### D — Decision Criteria
"When you evaluate options for this, what matters most to you?
How will you make the final call?"

### D — Decision Process
"Walk me through how a decision like this typically works at [Company].
What are the steps from here to getting started?"

### I — Identify Pain
"What's the cost of not solving this?
What happens if things stay the same for another 6 months?"

### C — Champion
"Who internally is most invested in solving this?
Who will drive this forward on your side?"

## Discovery Call Script

### Opening (2 min)
```
"Thanks for making time [Name]. 

Before I jump in — I want to make sure this call is 
valuable for you regardless of where we end up.

Can I ask — what made you agree to this call? 
What are you hoping to figure out today?"
```
*This immediately flips the dynamic — they're telling you what they need.*

### Situation Questions (10 min)
```
"Help me understand where you are today with [their challenge].
Walk me through what's happening."

"How long has this been a challenge?"

"What have you already tried to address this?"

"What worked? What didn't?"
```

### Impact Questions (10 min)
```
"What's the business impact of this problem?"

"If you could put a number on what this costs you — 
in time, revenue, or opportunity — what would that be?"

"What happens if this isn't solved in the next 6 months?"

"Who else in the business feels this pain?"
```

### Fit Questions (5 min)
```
"Based on what you've shared, I think we can help.
But before I get into what we do — is there anything 
that would make this a non-starter? Budget, timeline, 
anything like that?"

"What's your timeline for solving this?"

"Do you have budget allocated, or is this something 
you'd need to build a business case for?"
```

### The Pitch (5 min — only after you've listened)
```
"Based on what you've shared, here's how I think we can help:

[Client problem restated in their words]

What we do is [one-sentence solution].

The way it works: [3 bullet points — simple, outcome-focused]

A client similar to you — [brief case study in 2 sentences].

Does this feel relevant to what you're dealing with?"
```

### Close to Next Step (5 min)
```
"This feels like a strong fit based on what you've shared.

The logical next step is for me to put together a 
proposal for you. I'll cover [scope, timeline, investment].

Can we schedule [30-45 min] in the next few days to 
walk through it together?"
```

## Objection Handling

### "We don't have budget"
```
"Understood. Is budget the real constraint, or is it 
about priority right now? Sometimes they're different.

If the ROI was clear — say, 5x what you invest — 
would budget be found? Or is this genuinely not 
a priority right now?"
```

### "We're happy with what we have"
```
"Good to know. What are you currently using/doing for [this]?

[Listen]

What made you agree to take this call then? 
Is there something about what you have that's not quite working?"
```

### "Send me some information"
```
"Happy to. What would be most useful — a case study, 
an overview of our process, or pricing?

Can I ask — what would you need to see in the information 
to feel it's worth a follow-up conversation?"
```
*Always follow the information with a direct ask for the next meeting.*

### "We need to think about it"
```
"Of course. What specifically do you need to think through?
Is it the fit, the investment, the timing, or something else?

I want to make sure I give you whatever you need to 
make the right decision."
```

### "It's too expensive"
```
"Appreciate the honesty. Is the investment too high in 
absolute terms, or does it feel like the ROI isn't clear?

Let me reframe the investment: if we [deliver specific result], 
what would that be worth to [Company]?"
```

## The Non-Negotiables

- **Never pitch before you've listened** — earn the right
- **Take notes visibly** — shows you're serious
- **Always confirm the next step before hanging up** — a call with no next step is a lost deal
- **Follow up same day** — memory and momentum fade fast
- **Know when to walk away** — bad fits are expensive
